    Info

    The Game Museum is for anyone who is passionate about the history and culture of games. Also there are many different types of games to discover and learn The museum has around 231 game computers, home computers and arcade cabinets are ready to play, and 327 unique play areas. That makes us the museum with the largest arcade in all of Europe!

    History

    A garage box in The Hague is the place where the adventure started in 2008, with two friends Hasan Tasdemir and Pascal Rapailles. The garage quickly became too small while they moved to a shed on one industrial area. Unfortunately, the shed was not heated and everything had to be diverted to one in the middle of winter vacant Ministry of Agriculture. When this building also had to be cleared out, the current collection of 10 arcade video games was met some spare parts stored in an old classic car showroom. Because there was no room to renovate or play with the cabinets, they looked for a suitable solution room. It was found in 2011 on the Cobaltstraat in Zoetermeer. Among them, Hasan fell to 4 friends to rent a location and the adventure could grow into their own meeting place to practice their hobby.

    The Atmosphere Rooms

    As soon as you enter the museum, you are showered with everything the game has to offer culture so beautiful and impressive. Follow our timeline through the atmospheric rooms and discover the history of video games through the game changers, highlights and important moments in technology, artwork and culture within the game industry.

    The Lab

    The Lab is a dynamic space. From admiring game art to traveling through new ones worlds about the future of gaming. There is always something new to experience in the Lab: one time you will learn all about the history of gaming during a lecture and the other time we welcome you during one tournament or a meet-up. Experience the future of gaming, or shape it yourself in the Lab!
